Founder story

Built from nearly 25 years inside real kitchens.

86 Chaos was not dreamed up from a conference room or a software trend deck. It came from years of working through the same daily restaurant problems that managers, cooks, bartenders, and owners deal with every shift.

Why it exists

Restaurant chaos looks different everywhere, but the problems rhyme.

86 Chaos was created by someone with nearly 25 years in kitchens of all kinds, from major corporate operations to small independently owned restaurants and bars. That experience shaped the app around practical work, not theory.

Built for what managers actually chase

Prep that was missed. Items that should have been 86’d sooner. Ordering notes scattered between memory, paper, texts, and spreadsheets. Schedule changes. Event prep. Waste. Price changes. Staff reminders. The little things that become big problems when nobody has one clear place to see them.

86 Chaos is designed to bring those daily pieces together so independent kitchens can operate with more visibility, better handoff, and fewer avoidable surprises.

What shaped the product

Made for independent restaurants that need useful tools, not enterprise theater.

The goal is not to bury managers in software. The goal is to make the work easier to see, easier to hand off, and easier to act on during an actual shift.

Built on the line, not in a boardroom

Less chaos. More control.

After years of seeing the same problems show up in different kitchens, 86 Chaos was built around a simple idea: managers should not have to chase the whole operation from memory. Prep, 86s, ordering, schedules, reminders, events, invoices, waste, and manager notes should work together instead of living in separate corners.

Founder Beta exists so real restaurants can help shape the product while it is still being refined. Feedback from actual shifts matters more than polished guesses.
