Why it sells

Less tapping. Less hunting. Less “I’ll do it later.”

86Voice lowers the friction of using the app. A cook or manager can say what changed and let 86 Chaos route it to the right workflow.

Prep

Add or update prep

“Prep two pans of onions” can create or update a prep item with quantity, station, and target date instead of making someone dig through screens.

Done

Mark work complete

“Mark tomatoes done” can find the matching prep or task item and complete it, reducing stale lists and duplicate work.

86

Create 86 alerts

“We’re out of ribeye” creates a real 86 alert, checks affected menu items, and helps the right people know before guests order it.

Search

Search recipes and help

“Open beer cheese” or “search fryer cleaning” helps staff find specs, recipes, and instructions without scrolling through the whole app.

Remind

Create reminders

“Remind me to check the walk-in temp at 4” turns spoken follow-up into a reminder instead of another thing living in memory.

Safe

Built with guardrails

Voice actions respect permissions and can require confirmation for ambiguous or sensitive changes, so convenience does not become chaos with a microphone.

Real kitchen language

86Voice is built for how restaurant people actually talk.

It should understand short, imperfect commands, station language, quantities, common food names, “done,” “out of,” “add,” “open,” and “remind me” without needing robotic phrasing.

“Add clean fryer wall to weekly tasks.” “Mark bacon done.” “We’re out of wing sauce.” “Open the July schedule.” “What does beer cheese go on?” “Remind Jordan to pull soup at 3.”

Voice command flow

1Transcript appears so the user can verify what was heard.
286 Chaos detects intent: prep, task, 86 alert, reminder, search, or navigation.
3The app matches the right item, station, person, recipe, or screen.
4Low-confidence or risky actions ask for confirmation first.
5The completed action appears in the app with a clear result.
