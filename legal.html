Legal / Terms / Privacy

Important terms, privacy, AI, beta, and backup notes.

This page gives restaurants the plain-English essentials for using 86 Chaos. It is intentionally shorter than the internal legal packet and should be reviewed by a qualified attorney before paid launch.

Service overview

86 Chaos is a restaurant/kitchen management web app operated by Chilton App Works LLC for bars, restaurants, kitchens, and hospitality teams.

86 Chaos helps manage restaurant operations. It does not replace manager judgment, legal advice, tax advice, accounting advice, payroll advice, HR advice, food-safety obligations, employee notices, recordkeeping duties, vendor review, or business decision-making.

Beta and early-access use

During beta, founder beta, preview deployments, testing branches, early-access releases, and trial periods, features may be incomplete, unstable, delayed, unavailable, inaccurate, interrupted, changed, removed, or require redeployment.

Beta access does not guarantee permanent free access, lifetime pricing, feature availability, uptime, storage limits, scan limits, support level, or future integrations.

Customer backup responsibility

Customers and workspace owners are responsible for maintaining independent off-platform and off-site backups of business-critical records. This includes schedules, time punches, payroll-support records, invoices, inventory, recipes, legal records, employee records, tax records, vendor records, safety records, compliance records, and financial records.

86 Chaos backups, archives, recovery tools, provider recovery copies, and internal support tools are for service operations, diagnostics, security, and disaster recovery. They are not a substitute for the customer’s own required business records.

AI, Python intelligence, and automation

86 Chaos may include AI-assisted and Python-assisted tools such as 86Voice, AI Order Assistant, Menu Intelligence, invoice scanning, menu scanning, Manager Brief summaries, Python Order Intelligence, Python Ops Intelligence, automation scans, recommendation queues, diagnostics, and support tools.

AI and Python tools can suggest, explain, classify, extract, summarize, forecast, draft, alert, and report. Outputs may be incomplete, delayed, biased, incorrect, stale, or unsuitable for a specific restaurant workflow.

AI suggestions must be reviewed by an authorized human before business decisions or actions. Unless expressly enabled by a future written agreement, 86 Chaos does not automatically send vendor orders, spend money, approve payroll, approve timesheets, publish schedules, change wages, change permissions, change billing, delete records permanently, or modify customer data based only on AI output.

Privacy basics

86 Chaos is a business-to-business workplace tool. The restaurant or organization that creates a workspace generally decides what employee and operational information is entered and how the service is used.

Information processed may include account and staff information, schedules, shifts, time punches, request-off and availability records, role and permission data, prep lists, 86 alerts, inventory, recipes, invoices, vendors, menu data, maintenance records, sales or ledger entries, messages, reminders, attachments, plan and beta status, diagnostic data, audit logs, and device/push-notification data.

86 Chaos does not sell personal information and does not share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising.

Location, camera, files, and notifications

If enabled by the customer, location access may be used for time-clock punch verification. 86 Chaos does not intentionally run continuous background location tracking.

Camera, photo, and file access may be used for menu scans, invoice scans, profile images, message attachments, maintenance images, logos, or similar workflows. Users should avoid uploading unrelated sensitive information.

Notifications, reminders, push messages, AI outputs, scans, and automation may fail or be delayed and should not be the only operational safeguard.

Security and access

86 Chaos uses reasonable technical and organizational measures designed for a small business SaaS product, such as authentication, role-based access, server-side authorization, Firebase security rules, audit logs, backups, diagnostics, administrative controls, provider encryption, and security monitoring.

No system is guaranteed to be secure, uninterrupted, or error-free. Customers must use strong passwords, protect devices, limit elevated access, remove former workers promptly, configure permissions carefully, review admin access, protect exports, keep off-platform backups secure, and report suspected unauthorized access.

Plans, subscriptions, and payment

Founder Beta, trial, testing, free, discounted, or early-access terms may be changed or ended as described in the app, on the website, or in a separate written agreement.

If paid plans are activated, fees, renewal terms, cancellation terms, taxes, and payment-provider terms will be disclosed before payment where required.

Contact

Questions, privacy requests, security concerns, beta issues, support requests, or legal notices may be sent to support@86chaos.com unless another address is posted on the website or required by a signed agreement.
