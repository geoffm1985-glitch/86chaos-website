How it works

Start with one workflow. Expand into the whole operation.

86 Chaos is designed to become useful before it becomes complicated. A restaurant can start with prep, 86 alerts, reminders, and schedules, then add inventory, invoices, recipes, maintenance, and financial snapshots as the team gets comfortable.

Start smallAdd modules laterBuilt on the line

Simple rollout

A practical path for a real restaurant team.

The goal is not to dump software on the staff. The goal is to pick the most annoying workflow first, prove it helps, then build from there.

Step 1

Set up the workspace

Add the restaurant, staff, roster roles, prep stations, vendors, recipes, schedule basics, and the first workflows you want the team using.

Step 2

Run the daily shift

Use prep, line checks, reminders, 86 alerts, schedule visibility, and team notes so managers can see what is happening without chasing everyone.

Step 3

Use 86Voice when hands are busy

Speak commands like “mark bacon done,” “we’re out of chicken breast,” or “remind me to check the walk-in” and let the app route the action.

Step 4

Connect the records

Invoices, inventory, recipes, daily close, maintenance, labor, and financial snapshots create a cleaner operating history over time.

Why 86Voice matters in the rollout

Voice turns real kitchen speech into app actions.

It is one of the biggest adoption tools because it reduces tapping. Staff can keep prep, tasks, reminders, recipes, maintenance, and 86 alerts updated without stopping what they are doing.

Where AI fits in the day

AI helps before, during, and after the rush.

The point is not to replace the manager. The point is to make sure the manager sees what matters faster.

Before service

Prep prediction, event supply planning, order drafts, par warnings, and Manager Brief attention items help the day start cleaner.

During service

86Voice, 86 alerts, Menu Intelligence, status questions, reminders, and Kitchen Command Center help the team react faster.

After service

Burn/waste analysis, invoice warnings, recipe/menu cost signals, labor visibility, and close notes help owners improve the next day.

Background intelligence

AI scans and scheduled automation keep watch in the background.

Nightly Ops Scan, Morning Manager Brief, Invoice Price Watcher, Event Supply Risk Check, Waste Pattern Scanner, Par Recommendation Engine, Data Health Scanner, and critical alerts help managers catch problems sooner.

Manager attention

Instead of waiting for someone to notice, 86 Chaos can surface what needs review.

AI findings can turn into Manager Brief attention cards, recommendation queue items, copyable manager reports, CSV findings, or critical alerts depending on the issue.
