Features

Built around the way kitchens actually lose time.

Every module connects to a real shift problem: what needs done, what is out, who is working, what got ordered, what broke, and what the numbers are saying.

What actually sells the app

86 Chaos combines the tools independents usually piece together by hand.

Instead of listing every internal setting, the product story is simple: run the shift, protect food cost, reduce manager chasing, and keep the kitchen from relying on memory.

Smarter scheduling and availability

My Schedule, Full Schedule, Month View, Request Off, Availability, Schedule Builder, Schedule Copilot, coverage targets, templates, drag board, and conflict warnings help managers build cleaner schedules with fewer surprises.

Prep that does not duplicate itself

Prep lists, task workflows, Smart Prep Matching, and prep label printing help turn scattered kitchen work into visible action.

AI ordering and inventory visibility

Inventory counts, vendor/order tools, burn/waste logs, invoice tools, AI Order Assistant, price warnings, prep prediction, and event supply planning help managers order with less guesswork.

86Voice for faster work

Voice and typed commands help create reminders, prep, tasks, 86 alerts, events, availability changes, maintenance issues, waste logs, messages, and status answers without digging through screens.

Menu Intelligence and 86 impact

Menu dependency mapping shows what ingredients affect, what menu items are impacted by an 86, and which stock problems matter most.

Manager Brief and business visibility

Manager Brief, Kitchen Command Center, reminders, Event Calendar, Message Board, financial snapshots, labor visibility, and maintenance tracking help owners and managers see what needs attention.

Positioning

What 86 Chaos is not.

Not a POS replacement. Not enterprise accounting software. Not a generic task app wearing a restaurant hat. Not built to make the crew tap through twelve screens during a rush.

What it is

The operating layer between the POS, paper, and people.

86 Chaos is meant to catch the daily work that usually lives in memory, texts, notebooks, whiteboards, and “I thought somebody did that.” That is the goblin cupboard where margins quietly leak.
